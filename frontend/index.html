History of Astrology

Astrology is the study of the correlation between celestial movements and earthly events. Originating over 5,000 years ago in Babylon and Ancient India, it evolved from a method of tracking agricultural seasons to a sophisticated system of predicting human fate, karma, and psychological patterns.

Western vs. Indian (Vedic)

Western (Tropical)

  • Basis: Seasons (Sun's relationship to Earth).
  • Zodiac: Movable (Starts at Spring Equinox).
  • Focus: Psychological traits & Personality.
  • Planets: Includes Uranus, Neptune, Pluto.

Vedic (Sidereal)

  • Basis: Fixed Stars (Constellations/Nakshatras).
  • Zodiac: Fixed (Accounts for Earth's Precession).
  • Focus: Karma, Destiny, & Predictive Dashas.
  • Planets: 9 Grahas (Includes Rahu/Ketu).

Jyotisha: The Eye of the Vedas

Vedic Astrology is one of the six Vedangas (Limbs of the Vedas), crucial for understanding sacred texts.

  • Shiksha (Nose): Phonetics
  • Chhandas (Legs): Prosody
  • Vyakarna (Mouth): Grammar
  • Nirukta (Ears): Etymology
  • Kalpa (Hands): Rituals
  • Jyotisha (Eyes): Astronomy & Astrology

What is the Difference?
